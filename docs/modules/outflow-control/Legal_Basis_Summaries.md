## Key References:

### A. U.S. Border Doctrine:
- Government may conduct “routine searches” of belongings at borders without a warrant
- Digital searches must follow **limited scope** unless probable cause exists

---

### B. Applicable Laws:
| Law / Case | Relevance |
|----------------------|----------------------------------------|
| CBP Directive 3340 | Sets digital inspection rules (2018) |
| Riley v. California | Phones need warrant for deep search |
| U.S. v. Cotterman | Forensic device exams = “non-routine” |
| Export Control Act | Can block tech export to adversaries |

## Enforcement Requirements:
- All alerts must be traceable to audit log
- Legal status of device must be declared within 72h
- Human override must sign attestation on search legality
